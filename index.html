Understanding the Gita

Total: 18 Chapters | 700 Verses

The Bhagavad Gita is a sacred conversation between Lord Krishna and His friend Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. It contains timeless wisdom on dharma, karma, yoga, and devotion, addressing five fundamental truths: Krishna (God), the individual soul, the material world, action, and time.

Key Teachings of Bhagavad Gita (As It Is)

  • Five Basic Truths: Krishna (God), Individual Soul (jiva), Material Nature (prakriti), Action/Karma, and Time
  • The Soul is Eternal: The body is temporary, but the soul never dies and transmigrates
  • Karma Yoga: Perform your duty without attachment to results, offering all to Krishna
  • Bhakti Yoga: Pure devotional service is the highest path and ultimate goal of human life
  • Three Modes (Gunas): Goodness (sattva), Passion (rajas), and Ignorance (tamas) influence all actions
  • Supreme Surrender: The ultimate instruction is to abandon all dharmas and surrender to Krishna alone
  • Consciousness at Death: Your state of mind at death determines your next destination
  • Vedic Knowledge: Follow the parampara (disciplic succession) to understand spiritual truths
