Chapter 15: The Yoga of the Supreme Person

Purushottama Yoga (The Yoga of the Supreme Spirit) | 20 Verses

The Inverted Banyan Tree of Material Existence

Chapter 15 begins with one of the most striking metaphors in the Gita - the material world as an inverted banyan tree with roots upward and branches downward. The roots represent the Supreme Brahman, from which everything emanates, while the branches extend downward into the material realm. The leaves of this tree are the Vedic hymns, and one who knows this tree is the knower of the Vedas.

This inverted tree represents the topsy-turvy nature of material existence - what should be up is down, what should be down is up. Values are inverted, priorities reversed. The real source is above in the spiritual realm, but the material manifestation extends downward like an illusory reflection. Understanding this helps us see material existence in proper perspective - as a temporary, perverted reflection of spiritual reality.

Verse 15.1-2
"The Supreme Personality of Godhead said: It is said that there is an imperishable banyan tree that has its roots upward and its branches down and whose leaves are the Vedic hymns. One who knows this tree is the knower of the Vedas. The branches of this tree extend downward and upward, nourished by the three modes of material nature. The twigs are the objects of the senses. This tree also has roots going down, and these are bound to the fruitive actions of human society."

Cutting the Bonds of Attachment

The real form of this tree cannot be perceived in this world - no one can understand where it ends, begins, or where its foundation is. But this firmly rooted tree must be cut down with the weapon of detachment. Having done so, one must seek that place from which, having gone, one never returns, surrendering to the Supreme Person from whom everything began and in whom everything has continued to exist since time immemorial.

This teaching is both diagnostic and prescriptive. The diagnosis: we are entangled in material existence like a tree with deep roots of attachment. The prescription: cut the roots with the weapon of detachment, then seek the supreme destination. The process requires both severing false connections and establishing true ones - renouncing material attachment while developing spiritual aspiration. One without the other is incomplete.

Verse 15.3-4
"The real form of this tree cannot be perceived in this world. No one can understand where it ends, where it begins, or where its foundation is. But with determination one must cut down this firmly rooted tree with the weapon of detachment. Thereafter, one must seek that place from which, having gone, one never returns, and there surrender to that Supreme Personality of Godhead from whom everything began and from whom everything has extended since time immemorial."

Qualities of Those Who Attain the Supreme

Those who are free from false prestige, illusion, and false association, who understand the eternal, who are finished with material lust and freed from the dualities of happiness and distress, and who know how to surrender to the Supreme Person attain that eternal kingdom. This supreme abode is self-illuminated - it is not lit by sun, moon, fire, or electricity. Having reached it, one never returns to material existence.

This description shows that liberation requires both negative and positive qualifications - freedom from illusion and attachment, combined with knowledge and surrender. It is not merely escaping from material suffering but arriving at a positive destination of eternal existence in the spiritual realm. The emphasis on self-illumination indicates that the spiritual world is fundamentally different from the material - it is conscious, self-aware, and does not depend on external sources of light.

Verse 15.5-6
"Those who are free from false prestige, illusion and false association, who understand the eternal, who are done with material lust, who are freed from the dualities of happiness and distress, and who, unbewildered, know how to surrender unto the Supreme Person attain to that eternal kingdom. That supreme abode of Mine is not illumined by the sun or moon, nor by fire or electricity. Those who reach it never return to this material world."

The Living Entity: A Fragmental Part of Krishna

Krishna reveals that the living entities in the conditioned world are His eternal fragmental parts. Due to conditioned life, they are struggling very hard with the six senses, which include the mind. The living entity carries conceptions of life from one body to another as air carries aromas. When the soul acquires a new body and gives up an old one, it takes a certain type of ear, eye, tongue, nose, touch, and mind from the previous body.

This teaching clarifies the relationship between God and the individual soul. We are not God, nor completely separate from God, but eternal parts of God. Like sparks from a fire, we are qualitatively one with Krishna but quantitatively different. Our struggle in material existence comes from forgetting this relationship and trying to enjoy separately from the whole. Understanding our constitutional position as parts of Krishna provides both identity and purpose.

Verse 15.7-8
"The living entities in this conditioned world are My eternal fragmental parts. Due to conditioned life, they are struggling very hard with the six senses, which include the mind. The living entity in the material world carries his different conceptions of life from one body to another as the air carries aromas. Thus he takes one kind of body and again quits it to take another."

The Soul's Journey Through Bodies

As the embodied soul accepts new bodies, it gradually takes on the senses associated with those bodies. Presiding over ear, eye, touch, taste, smell, and mind, the soul enjoys sense objects. The foolish cannot understand how a soul quits one body and enjoys in another under the spell of the modes of nature. But those whose eyes are trained in knowledge can see all this clearly.

This passage describes the mechanics of reincarnation with remarkable clarity. The soul is the constant factor, while bodies and senses are variables that change. Those without knowledge cannot perceive this because they identify completely with the body. But those with spiritual vision can distinguish the eternal soul from the temporary vehicles it inhabits. This understanding transforms our relationship with birth, death, and bodily changes.

Verse 15.10-11
"The foolish cannot understand how a living entity can quit his body, nor can they understand what sort of body he enjoys under the spell of the modes of nature. But one whose eyes are trained in knowledge can see all this. The endeavoring transcendentalists who are situated in self-realization can see all this clearly. But those whose minds are not developed and who are not situated in self-realization cannot see what is taking place, though they may try to."

Krishna as the Source of All Energies

Krishna explains His various energies - He is the light in the sun, moon, and fire illuminating the whole world. Entering the earth, He holds all beings with His energy. He becomes the moon, nourishing all plants with juice. He is the digestive fire in the bodies of all living entities, combining with incoming and outgoing breath to digest all foods. He enters the hearts of all as the Supersoul, and from Him come remembrance, knowledge, and forgetfulness.

This teaching shows Krishna's intimate presence in all aspects of existence. He is not a distant God but actively present as the energy in light, the vitality in plants, the power of digestion, and the consciousness in our hearts. Everything we experience - from seeing sunlight to digesting food to remembering the past - is made possible by Krishna's energies. Recognizing this transforms ordinary experience into constant remembrance of the Divine.

Verse 15.12-15
"The splendor of the sun, which dissipates the darkness of the whole world, comes from Me. And the splendor of the moon and the splendor of fire are also from Me. I enter into each planet, and by My energy they stay in orbit. I become the moon and thereby supply the juice of life to all vegetables. I am the fire of digestion in the bodies of all living entities, and I join with the air of life, outgoing and incoming, to digest the four kinds of foodstuff. I am seated in everyone's heart, and from Me come remembrance, knowledge and forgetfulness. By all the Vedas, I am to be known. Indeed, I am the compiler of Vedanta and the knower of the Vedas."

The Two Persons and the Supreme Person

Krishna describes two types of beings in the world - the fallible and the infallible. All living entities are fallible, while in the spiritual world they are called infallible. But different from both is the Supreme Person, the Supreme Soul, who enters the three worlds and maintains them. Because Krishna transcends both the fallible and infallible, He is celebrated in the world and in the Vedas as Purushottama - the Supreme Person.

This three-fold distinction is crucial. There are conditioned souls in the material world (fallible), liberated souls in the spiritual world (infallible), and the Supreme Person who transcends both categories. Krishna is neither a great conditioned soul nor even a liberated soul, but the Supreme Controller, the source from which both realms emanate. Understanding this prevents us from thinking God is just a very advanced soul rather than a qualitatively different category of being.

Verse 15.16-18
"There are two classes of beings, the fallible and the infallible. In the material world every living entity is fallible, and in the spiritual world every living entity is called infallible. Besides these two, there is the greatest living personality, the Supreme Soul, the imperishable Lord Himself, who has entered the three worlds and is maintaining them. Because I am transcendental, beyond both the fallible and the infallible, and because I am the greatest, I am celebrated both in the world and in the Vedas as that Supreme Person."

The Perfection of Knowledge

Krishna concludes by declaring that one who is undeluded and who knows Him as the Supreme Person knows everything and therefore engages in full devotional service with all their heart. This, Krishna says, is the most confidential part of the Vedic scriptures, revealed by Him. By understanding it, one becomes wise and their endeavors are perfect.

The chapter thus provides complete knowledge - the nature of material existence (inverted tree), the process of liberation (cutting attachment and surrendering), the identity of the soul (eternal part of Krishna), the energies of God (present in all phenomena), and the ultimate truth (Krishna as Purushottama). One who understands these teachings has complete knowledge and naturally engages in devotional service, which is the perfection of human life.

Verse 15.19-20
"Whoever knows Me as the Supreme Personality of Godhead, without doubting, is the knower of everything. He therefore engages himself in full devotional service to Me, O son of Bharata. This is the most confidential part of the Vedic scriptures, O sinless one, and it is disclosed now by Me. Whoever understands this will become wise, and his endeavors will know perfection."

  • Weapon of Detachment: The tree of material attachment must be cut with determined detachment
  • Supreme Destination: Seek the place from which, having gone, one never returns
  • Self-Illuminated Abode: The spiritual world is lit by its own consciousness, not sun, moon, or fire
  • Fragmental Parts: Living entities are eternal fragmental parts of Krishna, struggling in conditioned life
  • Soul's Journey: The soul carries conceptions from body to body like air carries aromas
  • Trained Vision: Only those with knowledge can perceive how the soul transmigrates
  • Krishna's Energies: He is present as light in sun, vitality in plants, digestion in bodies, consciousness in hearts

  • Complete Knowledge: Understanding Krishna as Supreme Person leads to perfect wisdom and full devotion

Reflection

Chapter 15 provides a complete cosmology and soteriology - a map of reality and the path to liberation. The metaphor of the inverted banyan tree brilliantly captures how material existence is a perverted reflection of spiritual reality. Our task is not to improve this reflection but to sever our attachment to it and turn toward the source. The revelation that we are eternal fragmental parts of Krishna answers the question of identity - we are neither independent gods nor insignificant zeros, but conscious individuals eternally connected to the Supreme Person. Krishna's description of His omnipresent energies shows that spirituality is not about finding God in some distant heaven but recognizing His presence in every aspect of our experience - in the light we see, the food we digest, the thoughts we think. Most profound is the declaration of Krishna as Purushottama, the Supreme Person who transcends both the material and spiritual worlds, the source and sustainer of all existence. When we understand this truth without doubt, we naturally engage in full devotional service, which is both the means and the goal of spiritual life.
